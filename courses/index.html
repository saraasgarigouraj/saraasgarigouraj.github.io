ECON 1113 – Principles of Economics

Semester: Summer 2023 Course Description This course introduces students to the fundamental concepts of economics, including supply and demand, market structures, and the role of government in a market economy. Course Overview In this course, we explored micro and macroeconomic principles through lectures, Canvas discussions, and real-world case studies. Emphasis was placed on developing analytical thinking about economic decisions and policy. ECON 1123 – Principles of Economics

Semester: Fall 2023 Course Description An introductory course covering both microeconomic and macroeconomic principles, tailored for undergraduates from various disciplines. Course Overview Through active Canvas engagement, weekly assignments, and concept-based lectures, students gained foundational knowledge in economic reasoning and the functioning of economies at both individual and national levels. ECON 2843 – Elements of Statistics

Semester: Fall 2024 Course Description This course introduces statistical tools for analyzing data in economics, including descriptive statistics, probability, and hypothesis testing. Course Overview Students engaged with real economic data to build intuition around statistical methods and applied software (Excel/R) to perform data analysis. Weekly problem sets and guided labs supported skill-building. Course Materials Syllabus not available online. ECON 3113 – Intermediate Microeconomic Theory

Semester: Summer 2024 Course Description An intermediate-level course focusing on consumer and producer theory, market equilibrium, and welfare analysis. Course Overview We examined theoretical models of individual and firm behavior using algebra and calculus. Emphasis was placed on logical derivation and application of theory to policy and real-world settings.
